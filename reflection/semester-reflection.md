# Semester Reflection

ELYM316 helped me understand signals and systems more practically through MATLAB laboratory work. The mathematical theory became easier to interpret when I could turn equations into plots, inspect how signals changed, and compare numerical results with expected behaviour.

The FFT lab was especially useful because it showed how individual frequency components can still be identified in a noisy combined signal. The Gaussian-distribution lab connected probability theory with visual and numerical estimation, while the radioactive-decay exercise developed my understanding of exponential systems and Euler simulation. Working with step and impulse responses then brought together transfer functions, LTI behaviour, convolution, and time-domain analysis.

Overall, the module strengthened my confidence in moving between theory, MATLAB code, plots, and technical discussion. The strong laboratory results reflect consistent practical work, but the more important outcome was learning how to test and explain a signal model rather than treating the mathematics as an isolated calculation.
