# Fast Fourier Transforms - Lab Summary

## Aim

To demonstrate how an FFT can reveal sinusoidal components that are difficult to distinguish directly in a noisy time-domain signal.

## Method Summary

Three sinusoidal signals at 1 Hz, 5 Hz, and 10 Hz are generated with amplitudes 3, 2, and 1. The signals are summed, random noise is added, and a one-sided FFT magnitude spectrum is calculated. Peaks near the original frequencies are then inspected to estimate the recovered amplitudes.

## Learning Outcome

This lab made the relationship between time-domain waveforms and frequency-domain components more practical. It also showed why sampling rate, record length, FFT scaling, and noise level matter when interpreting a spectrum.
